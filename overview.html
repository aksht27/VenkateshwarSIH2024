Understanding Water Footprint

Water footprint is a comprehensive measure of freshwater use that looks at both direct and indirect water use of a consumer or producer. It includes the water used throughout the supply chain, as well as during the use of the product.

Why is Water Footprint Important?

Understanding your water footprint is crucial for identifying where water is important to your business and how it relates to the products you are making. It helps in managing water resources sustainably and reducing water-related risks.

Global Water Risks

The World Economic Forum’s Global Risks Report consistently lists water crises among the highest impact global risks. Until now, water has been treated as a free raw material. Today, companies are starting to realize that mismanagement of water can damage their brand, their credibility, their credit rating, and their insurance costs.

Unless you know how much water you are using – and how much is available in the river basins in which you and your suppliers operate – it is impossible to know whether you are using it sustainably or whether your business could face water-related business risk.
